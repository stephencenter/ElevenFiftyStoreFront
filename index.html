About Hamsters

Hamsters are small rodents that are often kept as pets. They hail from the deserts of Syria, and use their large cheek-pounches to store food. They are neither nocturnal nor diurnal - they are crepuscular. This means they are active during twilight.

Hamsters are naturally curious creatures and they love to climb, dig, and run. Because of this it's smart to have a very large cage full of fun things for your hamster to interact with such as chews and tubes.

Some hamsters are good in groups, and some are not. Syrians are very aggressive and terrirorial, and putting them in groups could end with them getting hurt. Dwarfs tend to be better in groups, but this is not always the case. Always do research before buying multiple hamsters.

Do not buy a hamster until you have these:

  1. A hamster cage
  2. A water bottle
  3. A food bowl and hamster food
  4. A wheel
  5. Aspen or Paper bedding
  6. Tubes, chews, or other toys

Shop for Hamsters

Roborovski Dwarf Hamster

Roborovski Dwarfs are small and quick. Full grown they are only 4 inches, and they live to be 3 years old. Good in groups.

Chinese Hamsters

Chinese Dwarf are small and love to dig, climb, and run. Full grown they are only 4 inches, and they live to be 2-3 years old. Bad in groups.

Campbell's Russian Dwarf

Russian Dwarfs are generally small, only 3-5 inches full grown. They live to be 2-3 years old. They are fine both in groups and on their own.

Syrian Hamster (Panda)

Syrian Hamsters are larger, generally 6-7 inches long. They are the most common type of hamster and an ideal first pet. Do NOT keep them in groups!
